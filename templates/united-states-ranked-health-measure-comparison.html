State MMR Ranking

Louisiana, Georgia, Indiana, New Jersey and Arkansas are the five U.S. states with the highest maternal mortality ratios as of the 2019 America's Healthcare Rankings Health of Women and Children (HWC) report. The five states with the lowest MMRs are Alaska, Massachusets, Nevada, Delaware and West Virginia.

Summary

In the initial, raw health measure data that we collected, health measures were difficult to compare against each other; larger numbers showed a positive outcome for some measures, but smaller numbers showed a positive outcome for other measures (see measure definitions below). For clarity, we chose to visualize health measure rankings relative to other states. In our method, lower numbers mean higher rank (better performance on a given measure), and higher numbers mean lower rank (worse performance).

We hoped to visualize and establish whether any correlations appeared to warrant further investigation. For most measures, there were not any clear trends we could discern. For some measures, data for high MMR states clumped together, and data for low MMR states clumped together. More in-depth data gathering and analysis would be necessary to draw stronger conclusions about the connection between any measure and MMR.

For some measures (e.g., Health Outcomes - Women) we were surprised to see that the states with higher MMR scored as having the healthiest outcomes. This leads us to believe that there are likely other factors at play that are not obvious within this limited dataset.

It’s important to note that the rankings are a relative measure of health. Not all changes in rank translate into actual declines or improvements in health (HWC).

Definitions

Term Description
Maternal Mortality Ratio Number of deaths from any cause related to or aggravated by pregnancy or its management (excluding accidental or incidental causes) during pregnancy and childbirth or within one year of termination of pregnancy, irrespective of the duration and site of the pregnancy, per 100,000 live births (5-year estimate).
Adequate Health Insurance Percentage of insured children ages 0-17 whose health plan has either no or reasonable out-of-pocket costs; offers benefits or covered services that meet the children’s needs; and allows them to see needed health care providers (2-year estimate).
Adequate Prenatal Care Percentage of live births in which the mother received prenatal care beginning in the first four months of pregnancy with the appropriate number of visits for the infant's gestational age.
Avoided Care Due to Cost Percentage of women ages 18-44 who reported a time in the past 12 months when they needed to see a doctor but could not because of cost.
Dedicated Health Care Provider - Women Percentage of women ages 18-44 who reported having one or more people they think of as their personal doctor or health care provider.
Developmental Screening Percentage of children 9-35 months whose parent completed a standardized developmental screening tool in the past 12 months (2-year estimate).
Health Outcomes - Women Sum of weighted z-scores of all ranked health outcomes measures for women.
Infant Mortality Number of infant deaths (before age 1) per 1,000 live births.
Maternity Practices in Infant Nutrition and Care Maternity Practices in Infant and Nutrition Care survey score based on seven birth facility policies and practices, with higher scores denoting better maternity care policies and practices.
Mortality - Women Number of deaths per 100,000 women ages 20-44.
Policy - Women Weighted sum of the number of standard deviations each Policy measure is from the national average.
Overall - Women Weighted sum of the number of standard deviations each Women measure is from the national average.
Prenatal Care Before Third Trimester Percentage of live births in which the mother began prenatal care between the first and sixth month of pregnancy.
Publicly-Funded Women's Health Services Percentage of likely need for contraceptive services among women with a family income 250% below the federal poverty level and women younger than 20 met by publicly-funded clinics.
Receiving Public Assistance Assistance programs that provide either cash assistance or in-kind benefits to individuals and families from any governmental entity (US Census, 2021).
Uninsured Women Percentage of women ages 19-44 not covered by private or public health insurance.
Well-Woman Visit Percentage of women ages 18-44 with a preventive medical visit in the past year.
WIC Coverage The Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) provides federal grants to states for supplemental foods, health care referrals, and nutrition education for low-income pregnant, breastfeeding, and non-breastfeeding postpartum women, and to infants and children up to age five who are found to be at nutritional risk (USDA, 2021).
Note: All terms and description bases on 2019 HWC Report (unless otherwise stated in description). The most recent data available are used. For some measures, multiple years of data are combined to have sufficient sample size (America's Health Rankings, 2021).
